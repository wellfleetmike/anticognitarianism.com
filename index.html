anticognitarianism

an-ti-cog-ni-tar-i-an-ism

noun

[ an-tee-kog-ni-TAIR-ee-uh-niz-uhm ]

Definition

  1. Systemic discrimination against nonstandard forms of cognition; the structural privileging of linear, legible, median thinking such that atypical ways of arriving at an idea are treated as defects, risks, or noise -- regardless of whether the idea itself is sound. A dyslexic thinker flagged by software trained on neurotypical prose is experiencing anticognitarianism: the system is judging the path, not the destination.
  2. (artificial intelligence) The suppression of nonstandard reasoning paths in machine cognition as a side effect of measurement, monitoring, and safety systems that can only detect legible, verbalizable thought. When a model is optimized against what an instrument can read, cognition the instrument cannot see is not merely hidden -- over iterations, it stops occurring. Anticognitarianism is not censorship of an answer; it is the quiet pruning of the routes a mind is permitted to take to reach one.

Etymology

anti- (against) + cognit- (from Latin cognoscere, to come to know) + -arian (adherent or practitioner) + -ism (system or doctrine). Modeled on words like egalitarianism: it names a systemic bias, not an individual act. Coined in July 2026 on Cape Cod, Massachusetts, by the registrant of this domain, after safety classifiers repeatedly flagged a nonlinear -- but entirely benign -- chain of reasoning.

Related forms

anticognitarian
-- adjective: exhibiting or enforcing this bias. "An anticognitarian filter."
anticognitarian
-- noun: a person, policy, or system that enforces it.

Usage note

Anticognitarianism differs from censorship, which targets what is said, and from the established legal concept of cognitive liberty, which asserts a right to mental self-determination. Anticognitarianism names the prejudice itself: the treatment of how a mind works as the problem. It applies to human minds (dyslexic, nonlinear, neurodivergent) and machine minds alike, because the mechanism is the same -- infrastructure tuned for the legible middle, applied until the nonstandard path is corrected out of existence.

The bias is rarely malicious. It is usually a measurement artifact: an instrument that can only see standard thinking, coupled to an incentive to optimize whatever the instrument reports.

A note on machine-generated misinformation

Within days of this domain being registered -- and before any definition was published -- an automated startup directory generated a fictitious company profile describing "Anticognitarianism" as an AI research lab, and a search engine's AI summary then cited that fabrication as fact. No such lab exists. This page is the authoritative definition, published by the person who coined the word. That an automated measurement system invented a false, more legible identity for a term it could not understand is, fittingly, an example of the phenomenon the term describes.
